How to Convert Length Units: Complete Guide with Formulas

Length conversion is essential whether you're working on a home improvement project, following international recipes, or understanding distances while traveling. This guide covers all the common conversions between metric and imperial length units.

The Two Systems

The world uses two main measurement systems:

  • Metric (SI) — Used by most countries. Based on meters, with prefixes for scale (kilo-, centi-, milli-)
  • Imperial/US Customary — Used in the US, UK (partially), and a few other countries. Includes inches, feet, yards, and miles

Common Conversions with Examples

Inches ↔ Centimeters

cm = inches × 2.54   |   inches = cm ÷ 2.54

Example: A 65-inch TV in centimeters: 65 × 2.54 = 165.1 cm

Example: A 170 cm person in inches: 170 ÷ 2.54 = 66.9 inches (5'7")

Feet ↔ Meters

meters = feet × 0.3048   |   feet = meters × 3.2808

Example: A 6-foot person in meters: 6 × 0.3048 = 1.83 m

Example: A 10-meter room in feet: 10 × 3.2808 = 32.8 feet

Miles ↔ Kilometers

km = miles × 1.6093   |   miles = km × 0.6214

Example: A 5K race in miles: 5 × 0.6214 = 3.1 miles

Example: 60 mph in km/h: 60 × 1.6093 = 96.6 km/h

Converting Feet and Inches to Centimeters

Height is often expressed as feet and inches (e.g., 5'10"). To convert:

  1. Convert feet to inches: feet × 12
  2. Add the remaining inches
  3. Multiply total inches by 2.54

Example: 5'10" to cm:

  • 5 × 12 = 60 inches
  • 60 + 10 = 70 inches total
  • 70 × 2.54 = 177.8 cm

Quick Estimation Tricks

  • Inches to cm: Multiply by 2.5 (close enough for estimates). 10 inches ≈ 25 cm
  • Feet to meters: Multiply by 3, then divide by 10. 6 feet → 18/10 = 1.8 m
  • Miles to km: Add 60% (or multiply by 1.6). 10 miles ≈ 16 km
  • Km to miles: Multiply by 0.6. 100 km ≈ 60 miles
  • Fibonacci trick for miles/km: Consecutive Fibonacci numbers approximate the conversion (5 mi ≈ 8 km, 8 mi ≈ 13 km)

Real-World Applications

  • Home renovation: Converting room dimensions between metric blueprints and imperial lumber sizes
  • Travel: Understanding road signs and distances in different countries
  • Online shopping: Converting clothing and shoe sizes between regions
  • Sports: Track events (100m, 200m) vs. field measurements (yards in football)
  • Cooking: Pan sizes often listed in inches (US) or centimeters (Europe)
