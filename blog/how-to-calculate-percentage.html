How to Calculate Percentage: Complete Guide with Examples

Percentages are everywhere — from shopping discounts and tax rates to exam scores and investment returns. Mastering percentage calculations is a fundamental math skill that you'll use daily. This guide covers all the common percentage problems with clear formulas and examples.

What Is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" literally means "per hundred." So 45% means 45 out of every 100, or 45/100 = 0.45 as a decimal.

The Three Basic Percentage Problems

1. Find a Percentage of a Number

"What is 20% of 150?"

Result = Number × (Percentage ÷ 100)

Solution: 150 × (20 ÷ 100) = 150 × 0.20 = 30

2. Find What Percentage One Number Is of Another

"30 is what percent of 150?"

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Solution: (30 ÷ 150) × 100 = 0.20 × 100 = 20%

3. Find the Whole When You Know the Part and Percentage

"30 is 20% of what number?"

Whole = Part ÷ (Percentage ÷ 100)

Solution: 30 ÷ (20 ÷ 100) = 30 ÷ 0.20 = 150

Percentage Change (Increase or Decrease)

Percentage change tells you how much a value has increased or decreased relative to its original value.

Percentage Change = [(New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value] × 100

Example: Price Increase

A product's price went from $80 to $95. What's the percentage increase?

  1. Find the difference: $95 − $80 = $15
  2. Divide by original: $15 ÷ $80 = 0.1875
  3. Multiply by 100: 0.1875 × 100 = 18.75% increase

Example: Price Decrease

A stock dropped from $120 to $96. What's the percentage decrease?

  1. Find the difference: $96 − $120 = −$24
  2. Divide by original: −$24 ÷ $120 = −0.20
  3. Multiply by 100: −0.20 × 100 = 20% decrease

Percentage Difference Between Two Numbers

When neither number is the "original," use percentage difference:

Percentage Difference = [|A − B| ÷ ((A + B) ÷ 2)] × 100

Example: Compare 60 and 80:

  1. Difference: |60 − 80| = 20
  2. Average: (60 + 80) ÷ 2 = 70
  3. Result: (20 ÷ 70) × 100 = 28.57%

Common Real-World Applications

Shopping Discounts

A $60 shirt is 25% off. What's the sale price?

  • Discount amount: $60 × 0.25 = $15
  • Sale price: $60 − $15 = $45
  • Shortcut: $60 × (1 − 0.25) = $60 × 0.75 = $45

Sales Tax

An item costs $45 and sales tax is 8.5%. What's the total?

  • Tax amount: $45 × 0.085 = $3.83
  • Total: $45 + $3.83 = $48.83
  • Shortcut: $45 × 1.085 = $48.83

Grade Calculation

You scored 42 out of 50 on a test. What's your percentage?

  • (42 ÷ 50) × 100 = 84%

Tips for Mental Math

  • 10% of any number — Move the decimal one place left (10% of 85 = 8.5)
  • 5% — Find 10% and halve it (5% of 85 = 4.25)
  • 15% — Find 10% + 5% (15% of 85 = 8.5 + 4.25 = 12.75)
  • 20% — Find 10% and double it (20% of 85 = 17)
  • 25% — Divide by 4 (25% of 85 = 21.25)
