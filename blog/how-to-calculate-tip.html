How to Calculate Tip: A Quick and Easy Guide

Calculating a tip shouldn't be stressful. Whether you're dining out, getting a haircut, or using a ride-share service, knowing how to quickly figure out the right tip amount makes the experience smoother for everyone.

The Basic Tip Formula

Tip Amount = Bill Total × (Tip Percentage ÷ 100)

For a $65 bill with a 20% tip: $65 × 0.20 = $13.00 tip

Total with tip: $65 + $13 = $78.00

In the United States, 20% has become the standard tip for good restaurant service. Many people consider 15% the minimum for sit-down dining.

Mental Math Tricks for Quick Tipping

The 10% Method (Easiest)

  1. Find 10% by moving the decimal one place left
  2. For 20%: double the 10% amount
  3. For 15%: add half of 10% to the 10% amount

Example: Bill is $47.80

  • 10% = $4.78
  • 20% = $4.78 × 2 = $9.56
  • 15% = $4.78 + $2.39 = $7.17

The Round-Up Method

Round your bill to the nearest easy number first, then calculate:

  • Bill: $47.80 → Round to $48 (or $50 for simplicity)
  • 20% of $50 = $10 (slightly generous, but easy)

The Double-Tax Method

In many US states, sales tax is around 8-10%. Simply double the tax amount for approximately 18-20% tip. Check your receipt for the tax line!

Splitting the Bill with Tip

Each Person Pays = (Bill + Tip) ÷ Number of People

Example: Dinner for 4

  • Bill: $120
  • Tip (20%): $120 × 0.20 = $24
  • Total: $120 + $24 = $144
  • Per person: $144 ÷ 4 = $36 each

Tip on Pre-Tax or Post-Tax Amount?

Technically, you should tip on the pre-tax subtotal since tax goes to the government, not the server. However, the difference is usually small (a few cents to a dollar), and many people tip on the total for simplicity.

Example: $50 subtotal + $4.25 tax = $54.25 total

  • 20% on pre-tax: $50 × 0.20 = $10.00
  • 20% on post-tax: $54.25 × 0.20 = $10.85
  • Difference: only $0.85

When Not to Tip

  • Counter service / fast food — Tipping is optional and appreciated but not expected
  • Countries where tipping isn't customary — Japan, South Korea, and many European countries include service in the price
  • When service charge is included — Check your bill for "gratuity included" (common for large parties)
