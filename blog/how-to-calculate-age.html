How to Calculate Age: From Date of Birth to Exact Age

Calculating age seems simple — just subtract birth year from current year, right? But when you need exact age in years, months, and days, or when you're dealing with legal age requirements, it gets more nuanced. This guide covers all the methods you need.

Simple Age Calculation

The quickest way to estimate age:

Age = Current Year − Birth Year (adjust if birthday hasn't occurred yet)

Example

Born: March 15, 1990. Today: May 28, 2026.

  1. Subtract years: 2026 − 1990 = 36
  2. Check if birthday has passed this year: March 15 has passed by May 28 ✓
  3. Age: 36 years old

If today were February 10, 2026 (before March 15), the age would be 35 — because the birthday hasn't occurred yet in 2026.

Exact Age in Years, Months, and Days

For precise age calculation, work backwards from today's date:

Example: Born July 20, 1995. Today: May 28, 2026.

  1. Calculate days: Today is the 28th, birth day is the 20th. Since 28 > 20: 28 − 20 = 8 days
  2. Calculate months: Current month (May = 5), birth month (July = 7). Since 5 < 7, borrow 1 year: (5 + 12) − 7 = 10 months
  3. Calculate years: 2026 − 1995 = 31, minus 1 borrowed = 30 years
  4. Result: 30 years, 10 months, 8 days

When the Day Requires Borrowing

Born: January 30, 2000. Today: May 15, 2026.

  1. Days: 15 < 30, so borrow from previous month. April has 30 days: (15 + 30) − 30 = 15 days
  2. Months: May (5) − 1 borrowed − January (1) = 3 months
  3. Years: 2026 − 2000 = 26 years
  4. Result: 26 years, 3 months, 15 days

Legal Age Considerations

Different jurisdictions have specific rules about when you "turn" a certain age:

  • Most countries: You turn a new age at the start of your birthday (midnight)
  • Some legal systems: Age changes at the exact time of birth
  • Korean age system: Everyone is 1 at birth and gains a year on January 1st (being phased out)
  • Chinese traditional age: Similar to Korean, counted from conception

Leap Year Considerations

If you're born on February 29 (leap day), age calculation has a quirk:

  • In non-leap years, most legal systems consider March 1 as your birthday
  • Some jurisdictions use February 28 instead
  • For exact day counts, remember leap years occur every 4 years (except centuries not divisible by 400)

Leap year rule: A year is a leap year if divisible by 4, except century years must be divisible by 400. So 2000 was a leap year, but 1900 was not.

Age Calculation for Specific Purposes

Insurance and Actuarial Age

Insurance companies often use "nearest age" — if you're within 6 months of your next birthday, they round up. A 34-year-old who is 7 months past their birthday would be rated as age 35.

School Enrollment Cutoffs

Most school districts have a cutoff date (often September 1). A child must turn 5 by the cutoff to start kindergarten that year. This means children in the same grade can differ by almost a full year in age.

Quick Age Estimation Tips

  • Same birth month as current: Check if the day has passed
  • December birthday, January today: Don't forget you just crossed a year boundary
  • Round numbers: Born in 2000? Your age equals the last two digits of the current year (after your birthday)
