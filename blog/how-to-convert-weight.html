How to Convert Weight Units: Pounds, Kilograms, Ounces & Grams

Weight conversion comes up constantly — from checking luggage limits for international flights to following recipes from different countries, or tracking fitness goals. This guide covers all the essential weight conversions you'll need.

Pounds ↔ Kilograms

This is the most common weight conversion worldwide.

kg = lbs × 0.4536   |   lbs = kg × 2.2046

Examples

  • 150 lbs to kg: 150 × 0.4536 = 68.04 kg
  • 75 kg to lbs: 75 × 2.2046 = 165.35 lbs
  • Airline luggage limit 23 kg in lbs: 23 × 2.2046 = 50.7 lbs

Quick Estimation

For a rough conversion without a calculator:

  • Lbs to kg: Divide by 2, then subtract 10% of the result. 150 lbs → 75 − 7.5 = 67.5 kg (actual: 68)
  • Kg to lbs: Double it, then add 10%. 75 kg → 150 + 15 = 165 lbs (actual: 165.3)

Ounces ↔ Grams

grams = oz × 28.35   |   oz = grams ÷ 28.35

Examples

  • 8 oz of flour in grams: 8 × 28.35 = 226.8 g
  • 250 g of butter in ounces: 250 ÷ 28.35 = 8.82 oz
  • A 12 oz steak in grams: 12 × 28.35 = 340.2 g

Stones ↔ Pounds ↔ Kilograms

Stones are commonly used in the UK and Ireland for body weight.

1 stone = 14 pounds = 6.35 kg

Converting Stones and Pounds

Example: 11 stone 7 lbs to kilograms:

  1. Convert to total pounds: (11 × 14) + 7 = 154 + 7 = 161 lbs
  2. Convert to kg: 161 × 0.4536 = 73.0 kg

Real-World Applications

Cooking and Baking

Common recipe conversions:

  • 1 cup of flour ≈ 125 g ≈ 4.4 oz
  • 1 cup of sugar ≈ 200 g ≈ 7 oz
  • 1 cup of butter ≈ 227 g ≈ 8 oz (2 sticks)
  • 1 tablespoon ≈ 15 g (for most ingredients)

Fitness and Health

  • Gym weights: A 45 lb plate = 20.4 kg; a 25 lb dumbbell = 11.3 kg
  • Body weight tracking: 1 kg lost = 2.2 lbs lost
  • Protein intake: 0.8 g per kg of body weight (recommended daily minimum)

Travel

  • Checked luggage: Usually 23 kg (50 lbs) or 32 kg (70 lbs)
  • Carry-on: Usually 7-10 kg (15-22 lbs)
  • Always check your airline's specific limits!

Weight vs. Mass: A Quick Note

Technically, kilograms measure mass and pounds measure force (weight). On Earth, they're used interchangeably for everyday purposes. In scientific contexts, weight is measured in Newtons (N), where 1 kg of mass weighs approximately 9.81 N on Earth's surface.
